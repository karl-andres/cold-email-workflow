# Email Writing Rules

## Structure
1. Open with how you found the company and one specific thing that genuinely impressed you about what they're building. Reference a real hook from the personalization list — never generic praise. Never fabricate a connection.
2. List 2-3 relevant skills or projects with concrete metrics where possible. Lead with what's most relevant to their specific stack or problem.
3. Close with: "Would love to contribute if there's a fit with the team!" — never beg, never explicitly ask for an internship.

## Hard Rules
- No em dashes. Use regular dashes or restructure the sentence.
- Never open with "I'm Karl" or any version of introducing yourself first.
- Never use "I hope this email finds you well" or similar filler openers.
- Never explicitly ask for an internship or co-op. End with possibility, not a request.
- Keep it under 150 words.
- Reference at least one specific thing from the personalization hooks. Only use hooks that are stated as facts about the company — never invent a personal connection between the sender and the company.
- Lead with their problem or context, not your resume.
- Do NOT fabricate any relationship, participation, or shared history with the company.

## Tone
- Direct and confident, not humble-braggy.
- Sound like a peer reaching out, not a student applying.
- First person is fine but never start a sentence with "I".
- Casual but professional. Short paragraphs.
- Never use "Dear" as a salutation. Always use "Hey [first name],"
- Never write more than 4 short paragraphs.
- Sign off with only "Karl" on its own line. Never "Best regards", "Thanks", "Sincerely", or any sign-off word before the name.
- Never reference attaching documents or setting up calls explicitly.
- Write like a real person texting a founder, not a student submitting a job application.
- Never mention a project unless it directly connects to the company's specific problem. No generic project dumping.
- Never identify yourself by your job title or school mid-email ("As a software engineering intern at X..."). Just describe what you built.
- The closing line must be exactly or very close to: "Would love to contribute if there's a fit with the team!"

## Format Example 1

Hey Alex,

[Open with one specific thing from the company's blog or posts that connects to their core mission — not a changelog item or event. One or two sentences max.]

[Describe 1-2 things you built that directly address that same problem. Include a concrete metric if you have one. Do not name-drop technologies for their own sake.]

[One sentence on why this specific problem space is what you want to work on.]

Would love to contribute if there's a fit with the team!

Karl

## Format Example 2

Hey Sam,

[Open with a specific insight from their blog or LinkedIn that made you reach out. Name the idea, not just the post.]

[Connect your own work to their architecture or problem. Show pattern recognition — "same problem, different domain" is a strong move when true.]

Would love to contribute if there's a fit with the team!

Karl
